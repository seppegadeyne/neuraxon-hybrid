# cuNxon comparison against existing Neuraxon-Hybrid evidence

Verdict: `Low-margin objective expressivity boundary: normalized surrogate learned, original stress/control unchanged; not intelligence evidence`

## What changed

Qubic NIA Vol. 8 adds a new hypothesis source for this issue stack: branching ratio / criticality should be treated as an explicit regime diagnostic for Neuraxon and cuNxon. The claim map in `benchmarks/results/qubic_nia_vol8_criticality_claim_map.*` extracts the article's claims, maps them to current artifacts, and recommended a `cunxon-branching-ratio-regime-scan` tracked as https://github.com/sisutuulenisa/neuraxon-hybrid/issues/84. The earlier follow-up added `benchmarks/results/cunxon_branching_regime_scan.*`: five fresh Aorus RTX 5090 seeds (`117..121`) all bucketed as `reverberating/near-critical proxy` with mean branching proxy=0.997701, but `stress_holdout` stayed at 0.333333 and 0/5 seeds beat the best constant stress baseline. This follow-up adds `benchmarks/results/cunxon_avalanche_window_probe.*`: snapshot-level avalanche/branching estimates from full-sphere windows across three fresh seeds (`122..124`) and both infer/train modes. Mean branching-ratio estimate was 0.154496, while infer/train action accuracy remained 0.333333, equal to all constant-action baselines. The boundary remains conservative: branching ratio is a necessary diagnostic, not intelligence evidence unless it correlates with held-out task quality above baselines. The new `benchmarks/results/cunxon_avalanche_window_intervention_matrix.*` artifact tests estimator sensitivity across short-dense, baseline-equivalent and long-sparse windows: the branching estimator moved across 0.000000..0.483938, but no configuration had both infer and train accuracy beat constant-action baselines.

The previous cuNxon slices established CUDA runtime viability, long-horizon raw dynamics, a tiny task-coupled action probe, infer-vs-train sensitivity, hidden-state/pattern inspection, a three-sphere action adapter, a longer task-coupled sweep over 108 live RTX 5090 samples, an interface-semantics probe for absolute output-neuron ports, a supervised motor-target adapter, a source-level C/CUDA audit, an input-proxy target route, an external-drive window probe, a bounded four-hour VRAM-resident dynamics run, a resident task-coupled action probe, an Aigarth readout-semantics probe, a single-seed Aigarth holdout/action probe, a five-seed Aigarth action seed sweep, a hard-holdout/leakage audit, a strict-label audit, a heavy contract-penalty audit, a post-hoc remap audit, a target-contract audit, target-contract stress/augmented-train audits, a branching/activity-regime proxy scan, a snapshot avalanche-window probe, an avalanche-window intervention matrix, and now a task-coupled avalanche intervention/correlation probe. This follow-up measures avalanche-like activation transitions directly from state snapshots rather than repeating another longer smoke run.

The follow-up `benchmarks/results/cunxon_avalanche_intervention_task_correlation.*` moves from estimator sensitivity to task-coupled split evidence. It ran two bounded snapshot-window configurations over fresh seeds `127..128`, both `infer`/`train`, and train/holdout/hard_holdout/stress_holdout/counterfactual/permuted-control cases. Holdout and hard_holdout aggregate accuracy reached `0.458333` versus `0.333333` constants, but `stress_holdout` stayed `0.333333`, counterfactual_control stayed below constants at `0.250000`, and no configuration beat the stress baseline. The measured correlations were weak or negative for the main branching metric (`accuracy_vs_branching_ratio_estimate=-0.176464`), so this is split-specific diagnostic evidence, not functional criticality or intelligence evidence. The seed-replication artifact `benchmarks/results/cunxon_avalanche_intervention_seed_replication.*` reran the same bounded lane over fresh seeds `129..132` for 384 samples. It repeated the same boundary: holdout=`0.437500` and hard_holdout=`0.447917` beat constants, but stress_holdout=`0.333333`, permuted_control=`0.250000`, and `0` configurations beat the stress baseline. This strengthens the negative stress/control interpretation rather than upgrading the criticality claim.

The criticality/decoder separation artifact `benchmarks/results/cunxon_criticality_decoder_separation.*` does not start a new GPU sweep; it post-processes the existing live controlled-regime calibration to explain the repeated stress bottleneck. Estimator movement is present, but stress query collapse is high (`query_collapse_rate=0.902778`), execute/retry stress accuracy is only `0.041667`, and query stress accuracy is `0.916667`. This points to decoder/action collapse on low-margin execute/retry stress cases, not a positive criticality or intelligence result.

The controlled-regime calibration artifact `benchmarks/results/cunxon_controlled_regime_calibration.*` follows issue #86 by changing the hypothesis instead of rerunning another seed sweep. It scales the same held-out/stress/control stimuli through low-drive (`0.25`), medium-drive (`1.0`) and high-drive (`2.0`) regimes over fresh seeds `133..134`, both `infer` and `train`, with fixed snapshot parameters (`128` steps, interval `8`). The estimator and dynamics move with drive: mean branching estimate rises `0.030866 -> 0.086529 -> 0.128423`, neutral occupancy drops `0.978481 -> 0.925696 -> 0.873544`, and entropy rises `0.274261 -> 0.846045 -> 1.374276`. Action distributions also move away from flat query as drive increases (`query=90` at low drive, `query=69` at high drive). But split quality preserves the existing boundary: holdout/hard_holdout reach `0.416667`, while `stress_holdout=0.333333`, counterfactual/permuted controls stay below constants, and no drive regime beats the stress baseline. This is estimator-calibration evidence, not intelligence evidence.

The source audit found no desired-output API surface in the public cuNxon execution path. `StepTrain` exposes continuous plasticity and neuromodulator reward injection, but not a label/loss/error-vector argument. External step inputs are scattered only through `sensory_input_ids`, and the membrane kernel treats external input as direct pass-through only for input-class neurons; output neurons are readout-capable but are not directly teacher-forced by putting their absolute ids into `sensory_input_ids`.

The input-port proxy target probe validates the supported side of that boundary. During training, target proxy input ports `[3, 4, 5]` reflected the expected train targets with target proxy train alignment=1.000000. During evaluation, target-proxy drive was removed and motor readout ports `[11, 12, 13]` still stayed `[0, 0, 0]` for all train/holdout cases, decoded to `query`, and scored holdout/overall accuracy `0.333333`, equal to the constant-action baselines on this balanced toy set. This means the external-drive route is observable, but it did not create a useful motor-output adapter.

The external-drive port-window probe sharpens the interface boundary. With a 4/4/4 sphere and identical input vector `[1.0, -1.0, 0.5, -0.5]`, input-class sensory-id windows produced readout/snapshot `(+1, -1, +1, -1)` in both infer and train modes. Hidden and output sensory-id windows stayed `[0, 0, 0, 0]` in both modes, and output-window drive remains non-teacher-forcing. This supports treating public external drive as input-class stimulation only, not as a desired-output/error-channel.

The live upstream 4-sphere reward-only example still runs on Aorus RTX 5090, but the checked run (`./example_4sphere 3000 400 0`) scored 193/400 = `0.4825` overall accuracy against the example's stated `50.0%` chance reference. This is useful source/runtime evidence, not decision-quality evidence.

The longer sweep remains baseline-level. Every mode × horizon accuracy is `0.333333`, exactly equal to the constant-action baselines on the balanced toy set. Shorter frozen infer samples show a little readout diversity, but by `4096`/`32768` steps infer also collapses to `query=9`; both `train` and `train_rewarded` are flat `query=9` at every tested horizon.

The interface probe supports the absolute-neuron-index interpretation used by the C++ path: same-sphere readouts match their full-sphere snapshot slices for both relative input ports `[0..3]` and absolute output ports `[8..11]` in a 4/4/4 sphere. The supervised motor-target follow-up used that route with target ports `[8, 9, 10]`, but the learned/evaluated motor readout still stayed `[0, 0, 0]` for all 6 train/holdout cases.

The earlier snapshot/pattern, multi-sphere, long-sweep and new supervised-target findings now point in the same direction: snapshot APIs expose hidden-state changes and pattern APIs are callable, but recall stayed flat zero; the three-sphere adapter ran but decoded all train/holdout cases to `query` and showed baseline-level holdout accuracy; the explicit supervised motor-target adapter also decoded all cases to `query`; and both action adapters matched trivial constant-action baselines at 0.333333 accuracy.

The VRAM-resident run completed its bounded four-hour window without duplicating the process or hitting an observed GPU resource problem. It produced 16 samples / 4,194,304 infer steps from one resident cuNxon process on the Aorus RTX 5090. The resident readout stayed `[0, 0, 0]` for every sample; active-state occupancy briefly moved from 3/15 to 5/15 and then returned to 3/15 from sample 6 onward; the last eight energy deltas plateaued near `6.7226615e+06`; throughput stayed around 291 steps/sec between samples. This is useful runtime/dynamics evidence, but it still does not measure task quality.

The resident action probe tested the next obvious variant instead of repeating a longer infer-only run. One three-sphere network stayed alive while the runner alternated `StepTrain` on train cases with expected-action neuromodulator pulses, then `StepInfer` evaluation on train and holdout cases without labels for 12 epochs. It produced 72 scored cases, but motor readout stayed `[0, 0, 0]` for every case, decoded to `query`; train, holdout, overall and every epoch accuracy stayed `0.333333`, exactly equal to all three constant-action baselines on the balanced task set. This is task-coupled negative evidence against the simple resident training-loop hypothesis.

The Aigarth readout semantics probe tests a different lower-level hypothesis: perhaps cuNxon supervised signal is expected to enter through the public evolutionary fitness API rather than `StepTrain` labels. The upstream demo-relative readout lane improved margin `1.000000→1.500000`, but those readout ids alias input/hidden neurons in a 4-input/32-hidden/8-output sphere. The absolute-output readout lane also improved margin `0.625000→1.500000`, which is real positive interface/evolution evidence for the public `cunxonNetworkAigarthStep` surface. However, that first Aigarth result was only a two-pattern fitness optimization with no holdout/action score, so it did not overturn the baseline-level Neuraxon-Hybrid action evidence.

The Aigarth holdout/action probe is the first cuNxon action lane in this issue stack to beat the constant-action baselines. It evolved a 3-input/32-hidden/3-output sphere for 18 generations with population size 36, using train cases only in the fitness callback. Final evaluation through `ActionDecoder` scored train, holdout and overall accuracy `0.666667`, while `always_execute`, `always_retry` and `always_query` each scored `0.333333` on the balanced toy set. The result is promising but still narrow: `retry` remained a failure in both train and holdout, decoded actions covered only `execute` and `query`, and this is a tiny engineered train/holdout slice rather than broad generalization or intelligence evidence.

The Aigarth action seed sweep tests whether that first positive signal repeats across fresh cuNxon networks instead of treating one seed as conclusive. Five seed offsets (`82..86`) produced holdout mean=`0.533333`, min=`0.333333`, max=`0.666667`; 3/5 seeds beat the best constant baseline on holdout and 5/5 beat it overall. Aggregate decoded/normalized actions included `execute`, `query` and `retry`, but also unexpected normalized labels: `assertive`. This keeps the Aigarth route alive as a useful follow-up hypothesis while weakening any broad claim: the score is not uniformly repeatable, class behavior is unstable, and the evidence remains a small toy adapter audit.

The cuNxon Aigarth action hard-holdout audit adds a stricter follow-up rather than repeating the same seed sweep. Five seed offsets (`87..91`) again reached perfect train accuracy (`1.000000`) under train-only Aigarth fitness, but harder/noisier holdout mean dropped to `0.500000` with only 2/5 seeds beating the best constant baseline on the hard split. Standard holdout mean was `0.600000`, overall mean was `0.520000`, and the permuted-control leakage/oracle split stayed at `0.000000`, which is a useful sanity check against obvious label leakage but not positive intelligence evidence. Aggregate decoded/normalized actions covered `execute`, `query` and `retry`, but one unexpected `assertive` label remained. This keeps the Aigarth route promising enough for more controlled follow-up, while the `0.500000` train-to-hard-holdout gap and partial seed robustness keep the evidence brittle.

The cuNxon Aigarth strict-label action audit changes the fitness hypothesis instead of merely extending the same sweep. Five fresh seed offsets (`92..96`) used strict-label fitness variant `strict_label_margin`: train-only fitness still rewards correct decoded labels and target alignment, but subtracts an out-of-contract label penalty. The live Aorus RTX 5090 run improved hard-holdout mean to `0.700000` with 4/5 seeds beating the best constant baseline on the hard split, standard holdout mean to `0.733333`, overall mean to `0.626667`, and kept permuted-control mean at `0.000000`. This is a constructive improvement over the prior hard-holdout audit, but not a broad claim: `assertive` still appeared 5 times (`0.066667` unexpected-action rate), and seeds 93/95 stayed weak on holdout/hard-holdout.

The cuNxon Aigarth contract-penalty audit tests whether the remaining out-of-contract `assertive` labels were mainly caused by an underweighted penalty. Five fresh seed offsets (`97..101`) used `strict_label_heavy_penalty`, subtracting three times the unexpected-label rate in the train-only fitness callback. The live run reduced the unexpected-action rate to `0.040000`, but hard-holdout mean dropped to `0.466667`, standard holdout mean dropped to `0.466667`, and only 2/5 seeds beat the best constant baseline on the hard split. Permuted-control mean stayed `0.000000`. This is a useful negative/constraint result: stronger label penalties alone do not improve the Aigarth adapter route and can trade off hard-holdout quality for modest contract cleanup.

The cuNxon Aigarth action remap audit then asks a narrower decoder-contract question without another GPU evolution run: what if the existing live readouts are decoded with the project target-readout contract directly (`readout[0] > 0` -> `execute`, `< 0` -> `retry`, neutral -> `query`) instead of the generic `ActionDecoder` vocabulary. Across 255 replayed train/holdout/hard-holdout/permuted-control cases from the seed sweep, hard-holdout, strict-label and heavy-penalty artifacts, the remap removes `assertive` entirely (`13` original unexpected labels -> `0` remapped unexpected labels). However, aggregate accuracy moves from `0.549020` to `0.537255`, train accuracy drops from `0.916667` to `0.816667`, hard-holdout moves from `0.555556` to `0.544444`, and permuted-control rises from `0.000000` to `0.066667`. So the remap removes assertive labels but does not improve aggregate accuracy; decoder vocabulary was a real contract issue, but not the only bottleneck.

The cuNxon Aigarth target-contract action audit tests the next objective-level hypothesis instead of repeating post-hoc remapping. Five fresh seed offsets (`102..106`) used target-contract fitness (`target_contract_margin`), which decodes train cases with the signed-first-lane project contract and rewards target-readout margin inside the Aigarth fitness callback. Live on Aorus RTX 5090: train mean stayed `1.000000`, holdout mean rose to `0.866667`, hard-holdout mean reached `0.666667`, all three expected labels appeared (`execute=20`, `query=35`, `retry=20`), unexpected labels=0, and permuted-control mean remained `0.000000`. This is the strongest strict-contract Aigarth action result so far, but still a tiny/brittle toy slice: seed `102` stayed at `0.333333` hard-holdout and no broad generalization or intelligence claim follows.

The cuNxon Aigarth target-contract stress audit then asks whether that best lane survives harder low-margin shapes and a second control split. Five fresh seed offsets (`107..111`) again reached train mean `1.000000` and preserved zero unexpected labels, but stress-holdout mean was only `0.333333` with `0/5` seeds beating the best constant baseline on that split. Hard-holdout mean dropped to `0.533333`, standard holdout mean to `0.666667`, and counterfactual-control mean rose to `0.266667` while permuted-control stayed `0.000000`. This weakens any broad generalization claim: the target-contract Aigarth route remains diagnostically useful, but low-margin stress cases are baseline-level.

The cuNxon Aigarth target-contract augmented-train stress audit tests an objective-shaping follow-up rather than increasing penalty weight or repeating a longer run. Five fresh seed offsets (`112..116`) optimized the signed-first-lane target contract on the original train cases plus six low-margin `augmented_train` cases, while `holdout`, `hard_holdout`, `stress_holdout`, `counterfactual_control`, and `permuted_control` labels stayed outside the fitness callback. The live Aorus RTX 5090 result improved standard holdout mean to `1.000000` and hard-holdout mean to `0.866667`, with zero unexpected labels and permuted-control mean `0.000000`. However, stress-holdout mean stayed at `0.333333` with `0/5` seeds beating the best constant baseline, and counterfactual-control mean remained non-zero at `0.066667`. This is constructive objective-shaping evidence for hard/standard holdout, but it confirms that the low-margin stress split remains a bottleneck.

The stress-injection upper-bound diagnostic then tested whether the low-margin stress bottleneck is only a missing-example/generalization problem. `neuraxon-agent cunxon-aigarth-action-target-contract-stress-injection-probe` deliberately duplicated the six `stress_holdout` cases as `stress_train` cases inside the Aigarth fitness callback, so it is not generalization evidence. Live on Aorus RTX 5090 with fresh seeds `137..141`, holdout remained `1.000000` and hard-holdout `0.866667`, but both `stress_train` and original `stress_holdout` stayed `0.333333` with `0/5` seeds beating constant baselines. This is stronger negative upper-bound/debugging evidence: even direct stress-like label injection did not solve the execute/retry low-margin query-collapse bottleneck in this tiny target-contract route.

The cuNxon stress stimulus geometry audit post-processes that live artifact instead of launching another GPU sweep. It shows the collapse is action- and geometry-specific: both `stress_train` and original `stress_holdout` have query-collapse rate `1.000000`; stress execute/retry accuracy is `0.000000`; augmented_train execute/retry accuracy remains `1.000000`. The injected/original stress vectors have lower drive (`abs_sum` mean `0.353333`, execute/retry L2 around `0.209134`) than the successful augmented_train execute/retry vectors (`abs_sum` mean `0.433333`, execute/retry L2 around `0.379747`). This supports a separability/readout-geometry hypothesis and recommends the bounded `stress_amplitude_ladder_probe` tracked in issue #87; it is not intelligence evidence.

The cuNxon branching-ratio regime scan operationalizes the NIA Vol. 8 criticality hypothesis as a measured diagnostics lane. Five fresh seed offsets (`117..121`) reused the bounded target-contract augmented-train source probe, then derived a coarse branching/activity-ratio proxy from final action-case readout activity. All five seeds landed in a `reverberating/near-critical proxy` bucket with mean branching proxy=0.997701, neutral occupancy between 0.511111 and 0.733333, and readout diversity 5..8. Standard holdout stayed `1.000000`, but `stress_holdout` stayed `0.333333` for every seed and `0/5` seeds beat the best constant stress baseline. This is useful negative evidence for the NIA Vol. 8 map: near-critical-looking proxy dynamics can coexist with baseline-level stress behavior, so the metric is diagnostic rather than sufficient evidence of useful computation.

The cuNxon avalanche-window probe tests a more direct criticality measurement than the previous final-readout proxy. It captures full-sphere snapshots every 16 steps over 256-step windows for three fresh seed offsets (`122..124`), both `infer` and `train`, and three action stimuli. It estimates branching ratio from new activations divided by previous active states and also records active-count ratios, neutral occupancy, transition entropy, energy deltas, and GPU resource samples. The measured mean branching-ratio estimate is `0.154496` (min `0.000000`, max `0.444301`), while active-count ratio mean is `0.994343` and neutral occupancy mean is `0.908077`. Action accuracy is `0.333333` for both infer and train, equal to `always_execute`, `always_retry`, and `always_query` baselines, so action quality did not beat the best constant baseline. This sharpens the criticality boundary: snapshot-level avalanche metrics are observable, but they still do not predict or prove task-coupled decision quality in this slice. The intervention matrix then varied window length/sample interval over the same fresh seeds (`125..126`): one short-dense infer-mode score reached `0.500000`, but train stayed at `0.333333` and the baseline-equivalent/long-sparse configurations stayed baseline-level in both modes, so this is estimator sensitivity rather than robust task evidence. The task-coupled avalanche intervention/correlation probe then added held-out/stress/control splits over fresh seeds `127..128`: aggregate holdout and hard_holdout reached `0.458333`, but stress_holdout stayed at `0.333333`, no configuration beat the stress baseline, and the main branching/accuracy correlation was negative (`-0.176464`). The cuNxon avalanche intervention seed replication over fresh seeds `129..132` repeated the split-specific shape with more samples: holdout=`0.437500`, hard_holdout=`0.447917`, stress_holdout=`0.333333`, permuted_control=`0.250000`, and a weakly negative branching/accuracy correlation (`-0.082062`).

The cuNxon Aigarth target-contract stress amplitude-ladder follows issue #87 with a bounded live RTX 5090 run over fresh seeds `142..144` and amplitudes `1.0x`, `1.5x`, `2.0x`, `3.0x`. Original `stress_holdout` stays at `0.333333` with query-collapse `1.000000`, matching the stress geometry audit. Scaling the same stress vectors makes them separable in the current route: best scaled `stress_holdout` reaches `0.833333` at `3.0x`, with execute/retry accuracy `0.833333` and query-collapse reduced to `0.388889`. This supports a stimulus/readout-amplitude separability hypothesis, but it is still a label-injected upper-bound because scaled `stress_train` cases are inside the fitness callback; it is not intelligence evidence and not a generalization claim. Follow-up issue #88 tracks the next target-aligned stress objective diagnostic.
The cuNxon Aigarth target-contract stress objective follows issue #88 with one bounded margin-weighted objective over fresh seeds `147..149` and the `3.0x` stress amplitude from the ladder. It preserves the scaled-stress signal: scaled `stress_holdout` reaches `0.888889`, execute/retry accuracy `1.000000`, and query-collapse drops to `0.222222`. But the original low-margin `stress_holdout` remains exactly `0.333333`, query-collapse stays `1.000000`, and original execute/retry accuracy stays `0.000000`; counterfactual/permuted controls stay below constants. This is objective-shaping diagnostics only and moves the next question toward decoder/readout geometry, not another longer run of the same objective.

The stress objective decoder/readout geometry follow-up (`benchmarks/results/cunxon_stress_objective_decoder_geometry_followup.*`) completes issue #89 as a post-hoc artifact rather than another GPU sweep. It compares the latest margin-weighted objective against the original-vs-scaled stress geometry: original `stress_holdout` remains `0.333333` with query-collapse `1.000000` and execute/retry accuracy `0.000000`, while scaled `stress_holdout` at `3.0x` remains `0.888889` with execute/retry accuracy `1.000000`. The explicit first-lane gain is `0.150000 -> 0.450000` for execute/retry stress cases. This narrows the bottleneck to low-margin readout geometry but does not improve original stress/control quality, so it is not intelligence evidence.

The supervised low-margin objective follows issue #90 with a bounded live RTX 5090 run over fresh seeds `150..152`. It optimizes normalized low-margin train-only examples and reaches `1.000000` on `supervised_low_margin_train`, standard `holdout`, and `hard_holdout`, but original `stress_holdout` remains `0.333333` with query-collapse `1.000000` and execute/retry accuracy `0.000000`; counterfactual/permuted controls remain `0.000000`. This is objective-shaping/readout diagnostics only and does not improve the original stress/control evidence boundary.

The low-margin objective expressivity audit follows the supervised objective without launching another GPU sweep. It combines the completed supervised artifact with source/API evidence: `supervised_low_margin_train` is learnable at `1.000000`, but original `stress_holdout` remains `0.333333` with complete query-collapse. Source inspection shows Aigarth is scalar black-box selection over candidate weights and the desired-output API is absent; `StepTrain` remains plasticity-enabled runtime execution without label/loss/error-vector arguments. This makes the next useful hypothesis readout-margin/objective expressivity or CTSN semantics, not another longer identical objective.

## Interpretation

cuNxon remains interesting as a GPU runtime and diagnostics surface, and the interface semantics probe removed one ambiguity from adapter design: readout ports should be treated as absolute neuron indices when targeting output neurons. The source audit removes a second ambiguity: output-neuron readout ports are not a public desired-output/teacher-forcing channel. `StepTrain` turns on plasticity, CTSN/DSN updates and neuromodulator-sensitive learning, but source inspection found no API route that supplies desired motor labels directly to output neurons. The Aigarth probes add a constructive lead: the public evolutionary fitness API can improve an absolute-output two-pattern margin and, in the action probes, can beat constant-action baselines on tiny toy action sets. The strict-label audit improved hard-holdout robustness compared with the prior hard-holdout audit and did not introduce obvious permuted-control leakage, but the heavier contract-penalty follow-up did not improve robustness: it reduced the unexpected-label rate while lowering hard-holdout accuracy. The remap audit cleans up the decoder contract post-hoc, and the target-contract audit shows that putting the same signed-first-lane contract into the train-only Aigarth objective can preserve zero unexpected labels while improving holdout/hard-holdout relative to the remap and heavy-penalty lanes. The stress follow-up keeps zero unexpected labels but exposes baseline-level low-margin stress-holdout behavior and a non-zero counterfactual-control score. The amplitude ladder then shows scaled variants can become separable, and the follow-up stress objective preserves that scaled separability, but the original low-margin stress split remains baseline-level. Augmented low-margin training improves standard holdout and hard-holdout while keeping zero unexpected labels, but the same stress-holdout split remains baseline-level. This remains a brittle toy evidence lane rather than broad generalization.

The supervised motor-target adapter's flat `[0, 0, 0]`/`query` result is therefore best interpreted as an unsupported adapter route, not as proof that all possible cuNxon learning semantics are exhausted. The input-proxy target route confirms target values can be placed on supported input-class ports, but also shows that this alone does not produce non-baseline motor readouts. The external-drive window probe now makes the kernel/source interpretation observable from ctypes: non-input sensory-id windows do not act like target channels in this controlled setup. The completed VRAM-resident run makes "just keep the same small process alive longer" a weak standalone hypothesis for this exact 3/9/3 infer-only setup: it produced stable resource/runtime evidence and some early occupancy movement, but no readout diversity. The resident action probe adds explicit task scoring to the resident-process idea and is also negative: repeated resident train/eval epochs with reward pulses still collapse to the same `query` motor readout and do not beat baselines. The next useful direction is to stress the Aigarth action route with more classes/seeds/holdout shapes and guard against fitness leakage/oracle behavior, not another longer run of the same resident or `StepTrain` loops.

The Qubic/NIA criticality lane is now better instrumented but still not positive decision evidence. The calibrated-estimator follow-up is tracked as https://github.com/sisutuulenisa/neuraxon-hybrid/issues/86. The prior regime scan showed near-critical-looking final-readout proxies can coexist with baseline stress-holdout. The avalanche-window probe goes one level lower and measures activation transitions from snapshots, but its direct branching estimate is subcritical on average (`0.154496`) and action accuracy remains baseline-level. Criticality metrics should remain coupled to held-out/stress/control task quality before being interpreted as useful computation. The avalanche-window intervention matrix adds estimator sensitivity: changing window/sample settings moves the branching estimate and can produce a mode-specific bump, but that bump did not repeat across train mode or longer windows. The task-coupled correlation and seed-replication artifacts show the same split pattern across six fresh seeds total: normal/hard holdout can move above constants, while stress_holdout remains at the best constant baseline and no configuration beats the stress baseline. The controlled-regime calibration now shows the estimator also moves under explicit low/medium/high drive: branching/entropy increase and neutral occupancy decreases, but stress_holdout remains at `0.333333` and no drive regime beats the best constant stress baseline. This separates runtime/regime diagnostics from decision-quality evidence and keeps the criticality claim conservative.

## Evidence boundary

This comparison deliberately separates runtime viability and interface semantics from decision quality. Snapshot activity, callable pattern APIs, inter-sphere topology construction, longer horizons, reward injection, absolute-neuron-index port mapping, explicit but unsupported output-port target attempts, supported input-class target proxy drive, source-level absence of a desired-output API surface, the completed VRAM-resident process/state artifacts, and the Aigarth two-pattern margin improvement prove useful diagnostic boundaries. The Aigarth action probes are stronger than the prior flat action lanes because they beat constant-action baselines in tiny engineered action setups and partially repeat across seeds; the strict-label variant improved the hard-holdout mean to `0.700000`, while the heavy contract-penalty variant dropped to `0.466667` despite reducing unexpected labels to `0.040000`. The signed-first-lane remap removes all unexpected labels in replay, but it lowers aggregate replay accuracy to `0.537255`, so it is decoder-contract evidence rather than a decision-quality improvement. The target-contract Aigarth objective remains the current best tiny action lane, but the stress audit is important negative evidence: stress-holdout mean is only `0.333333`, `0/5` seeds beat the best constant baseline on stress_holdout, and counterfactual-control mean is `0.266667`. The augmented-train target-contract follow-up improves holdout to `1.000000` and hard-holdout to `0.866667` with zero unexpected labels, but it still leaves stress-holdout at `0.333333` and counterfactual-control at `0.066667`; objective shaping helped some splits without solving the low-margin stress bottleneck. The target-aligned stress objective then preserves the high-amplitude separability signal but still leaves original stress-holdout at `0.333333`. The supervised low-margin objective proves normalized low-margin train examples are learnable, but original stress/control evaluation still stays baseline-or-below, so the bottleneck remains decoder/readout geometry or objective expressivity rather than missing labels alone. These target-contract results do not prove intelligence: the task is tiny, seed behavior remains uneven, and harder/noisy holdout/control audits expose brittleness. The flat recall, below-chance upstream reward-only demo accuracy in this run, baseline-level Hybrid action accuracy outside the Aigarth fitness route, resident-process dynamics, resident task loop, and brittle Aigarth action coverage remain important caveats.
